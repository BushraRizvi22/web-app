Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Founder of Pakistan

Early Life

Muhammad Ali Jinnah was born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, British India (now Pakistan). He was the eldest of seven children in a mercantile family. Jinnah was educated at the Bombay University and Lincoln's Inn in London, where he trained as a barrister.

Political Career

Jinnah was a prominent leader of the All-India Muslim League and played a pivotal role in the creation of Pakistan. His vision and leadership were instrumental in the partition of India and the establishment of Pakistan as a separate nation in 1947.

Legacy

Jinnah served as Pakistan's first Governor-General until his death on September 11, 1948. He is revered in Pakistan as the 'Father of the Nation' and his birthday is celebrated as a national holiday.

"With faith, discipline and selfless devotion to duty, there is nothing worthwhile that you cannot achieve." - Muhammad Ali Jinnah
